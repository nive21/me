Expanding the Market Reach of Dhyana

Context

Dhyana is a smart ring that leverages Heart Rate Variability (HRV) to evaluate the effectiveness of meditation sessions. Despite receiving positive feedback from users, the product's reach was predominantly among early adopters. Our objective was to boost sales by targeting a broader market.


Results
  • The user base surged from fewer than 500 to over 50,000 within a year.
  • The website redesign led to approximately 50% reduction in the bounce rate.
  • Sales of the product increased threefold.
My role

During the app revamp, I collaborated with my colleague and UX Designer, Devprakash. Together, we conducted interviews and developed prototypes. I spearheaded the review of analytics and identified opportunities for both the app and the website.

Our ring sales tripled times in one year.

Users lacked a fundamental understanding of the product's core value.

Problem Identification

While conversing with customers, I discovered a significant misunderstanding regarding the term "mindful minutes." Many users mistakenly believed it referred to the total duration of their meditation, rather than the specific time they were calm and focused. While it was clear that we needed to redefine this term and its presentation, it also became evident that users lacked a fundamental understanding of the product's core value.

In the updated user flow, we communicate the product's benefits at the outset, offer a short trial session, and make registration a requirement only for accessing detailed reports.

We spent less on ads to convert app users into ring buyers than on ads aimed directly at selling rings.

Action

To address these challenges, we launched an experiment that allowed potential customers to use their phone’s camera to trial the meditation tracking feature before committing to a purchase. This strategy not only showcased the technology but also positioned the ring as a premium upgrade for an enhanced experience. Additionally, I analyzed the app’s analytics and pinpointed a high drop-off rate during the pre-sign-up phase and few users ever attempting a session. We revamped the onboarding process to clearly communicate the product's benefits at the outset, offered a short trial session, and made registration a requirement only for accessing detailed reports.

Outcome

The introduction of a trial experience using just the mobile app reduced our customer acquisition costs. We spent less on ads to convert app users into ring buyers than on ads aimed directly at selling rings. The trial also helped demonstrate the product's value. However, the process was not without its challenges and learnings. Initially, a lack of clarity in the onboarding process led to a poor user experience teaching me the importance of thorough testing. I also learned to work with multiple teams while empathizing with their timelines, requirements, and constraints.

Within a year, we were able to expand the user base from fewer than 500 to over 50,000 and increase sales threefold.
